Managers, Not Owners

Psalms 50:8-15 KJV
8 I will not reprove thee for thy sacrifices or thy burnt offerings, to have been continually before me.
9 I will take no bullock out of thy house, nor he goats out of thy folds.
10 For every beast of the forest is mine, and the cattle upon a thousand hills.
11 I know all the fowls of the mountains: and the wild beasts of the field are mine.
12 If I were hungry, I would not tell thee: for the world is mine, and the fulness thereof.
13 Will I eat the flesh of bulls, or drink the blood of goats? but in sincerity of obedience
14 Offer unto God thanksgiving; and pay thy vows unto the most High:
15 And call upon me in the day of trouble: I will deliver thee, and thou shalt glorify me.
God never forgets a vow — but sometimes, His people do. In Psalm 50, the Lord rebukes Israel not for their lack of sacrifices, but for their lack of sincerity. They were giving externally, but not obeying internally. God is not impressed by what we bring to the altar

if our hearts are absent from the offering. They gave their offerings, yes — but they did it to perform, not to please; to appear spiritual before men, rather than to be sincere before God. That’s why the Lord reminded them, “I will not reprove thee for thy sacrifices… For every beast of the forest is mine.” (vv. 8–10) God was saying, “I don’t need your offerings — I want your obedience.

I don’t need your things — I want your thanksgiving.” You see, God is much more interested in the intents of the heart than in the impressions we make before men. He desires authentic devotion, not artificial display. We may fool others with our appearance, but we can never fool the Almighty — because He weighs the motives before He watches the motions. And that’s what true stewardship is about — not ownership, but obedience; not performance, but purity of heart; not reputation, but relationship with the God who owns it all.

When we fulfill our tithes, offerings, missions, and firstfruits, we acknowledge that we are not owners, but stewards of God’s blessings.

I. GOD’S POSSESSION

- He Owns Everything We Have (vv. 10–12)
“For every beast of the forest is mine, and the cattle upon a thousand hills.”

A. The Source of Our Substance

Deuteronomy 8:18 — “But thou shalt remember the Lord thy God: for it is He that giveth thee power to get wealth.” All our ability to earn, plan, or save comes from God.

B. The Sovereignty of Our Supplier

James 1:17 — “Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above.” God does not depend on us; rather, we depend on Him for everything.

C. The Sincerity of our Stewardship

Everything belongs to God (possession), We are only managers (stewardship), and God cares about our sincerity, not just our sacrifice. 1 Corinthians 4:2 — “Moreover it is required in stewards, that a man be found faithful.” We are called not to own, but to honor; not to keep, but to commit.
