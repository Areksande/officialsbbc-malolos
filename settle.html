Don’t Settle for Less Than God’s Best

Jeremiah 29:11 For I know the thoughts that I think toward you, saith the LORD, thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you an expected end.
Introduction: God Has a Specific Plan for You Not random. Not accidental. Not forgotten. You are not overlooked. You are designed. God Thinks About You The Creator of heaven is mindful of your life. If God is thinking about your future, why settle for fear and doubt? God wants to perform financial miracles in your life as well, and He is capable of doing infinitely more than you can ask, think, or dream. Ephesians 3:20 Now unto him that is able to do exceeding abundantly above all that we ask or think, according to the power that worketh in us, And not only is He capable, He also desires to perform those miracles for you. -xBut the problem often times we limit God by how little we believe Him for, and if we settle for less than His best, then less is all He can do. I'm going to do all I can to convince you not to settle for less. Let's go after God's highest and best in the realm of prosperity! I. Pray With Understanding – Know What You’re Asking 📖 Matthew 6:9–10 “Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven.” When we pray this, we are saying: * “Lord, let heaven’s will happen in my life.” * “What is normal in heaven, make it normal in my home.” * “What You desire above, do it here below.” Ask yourself: * Is there lack in heaven? * Is there bankruptcy in heaven? * Is there hunger, fear, or unpaid bills in heaven? 📖 Revelation 7:16–17 “They shall hunger no more… neither thirst… and God shall wipe away all tears.” 📖 John 14:2–3 “In my Father’s house are many mansions…” ✨ Truth: There is no lack in heaven.  If we pray for heaven’s will on earth, then we must believe God desires blessing— not bondage—for His children. II. Jesus Came to Give Abundant Life 📖 John 10:10 “I am come that they might have life, and that they might have it more abundantly.” Jesus did not die for: -x Barely-make-it living -x Constant defeat -x A defeated mindset He came for: * Abundant life * Overflowing grace * Victorious living Abundant life is not just money—it includes: * Peace * Purpose * Provision * Power to serve Don’t limit God by small thinking: “Hanggang dito lang ako.”  “Ito lang ang kaya ko.”  “Matanda na ako.”  “Bata lang ako.” But the real question is:  Is there anything too hard for God? III. God Is a Good Father 📖 Matthew 7:11 “How much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask him?” 📖 Psalm 35:27 “The LORD… hath pleasure in the prosperity of his servant.” God is not a cruel master.  He is a loving Father. As parents, we want our children to: * Succeed * Be secure * Be blessed How much more does God want that for His children? He does not enjoy seeing His children struggle forever.  He delights in their growth and blessing. IV. Prosperity With Purpose: Generosity True prosperity produces generosity. 📖 2 Corinthians 8:7 “See that ye abound in this grace also.” The happiest believers are generous believers. * Stingy hearts are miserable hearts. * Giving hearts are joyful hearts. * A giving church is a growing church. “Beginning with Abraham, God’s purpose has never changed.” Genesis 12:2 And I will make of thee a great nation, and I will bless thee, and make thy name great; and thou shalt be a blessing: Galatians 3:14 (KJV) — “That the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Jesus Christ…” God’s redemptive purpose to bless others through His people has not changed. 2 Corinthians 9:8 (KJV) “And God is able to make all grace abound toward you; that ye, always having all sufficiency in all things, may abound to every good work.” 👉 God gives sufficiency so we can abound in good works. 📖 Luke 6:38 (KJV) “Give, and it shall be given unto you; good measure, pressed down, and shaken together, and running over…” 👉 Blessing flows through giving. V. Biblical Example of Firstfruits Giving In the time of Hezekiah, the people obeyed God in giving. 📖 2 Chronicles 31:21 “He did it with all his heart, and prospered.” Their giving was: * ✅ Good * ✅ Right * ✅ True 📖 2 Chronicles 31:5 They brought their firstfruits abundantly. The result? 📖 2 Chronicles 31:10 “The LORD hath blessed his people; and that which is left is this great store.” When God blesses, He blesses in overflow. VI. The Early Church Practiced It 📖 Acts 4:34–35 “Neither was there any among them that lacked…” The New Testament church: * Gave sacrificially * Shared generously * Lacked nothing Giving is not a modern idea.  It is a Bible principle. VII. The Principle of Firstfruits What is Firstfruits?  Giving God first and best—not leftovers. Minimum may be the first month salary equivalent. But ask yourself: * Does God deserve the minimum? * Or does He deserve our best? You may start small—but don’t stay small. 💡 Principle: The bigger the sacrifice, the bigger the blessing. You cannot out-give God. VIII. Put God First in Everything If you want God to bless: * 🏠 Your family → Put Him first. * ❤️ Your relationship → Put Him first. * 💰 Your finances → Put Him first. * ⛪ Your ministry → Put Him first. Giving is not loss—it is investment. When you put God first: * He protects what you entrust to Him. * He provides beyond what you expect. * He prospers what you commit to Him. 🔥 Final Challenge Don’t live in God’s permissive will  When you can live in God’s perfect will. Stay in the Word until faith rises inside you. Refuse lack. Refuse small thinking.  Refuse settling. Declare in faith: “God takes pleasure in my prosperity.  He has given me abundant life.  I will not settle for less than His best.” Conclusion Giving is: * Good * Right * Truth Not giving, after knowing the truth, is choosing less than God’s design. So today ask yourself: 👉 Will I limit God? 👉 Or will I trust Him fully? Don’t settle for less than God’s best.
